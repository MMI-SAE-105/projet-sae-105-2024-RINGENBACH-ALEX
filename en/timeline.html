Timeline

Creation of the first automaton by Philo of Byzantium in 300 BC he only knew how to serve water.
1495: Leonardo da Vinci designs a mechanical knight capable of basic movements, considered one of the first examples of a humanoid robot.
1738: Jacques de Vaucanson, a French engineer, builds several automatons, including a famous mechanical duck capable of simulating digestive movements.
1800s: Automata become popular in royal houses and at exhibitions, with creations such as those of Pierre Jaquet-Droz, a Swiss clockmaker who creates complex mechanical dolls that can write or draw.
1956: The first robotics company, Unimation, is founded by George Devol and Joseph Engelberger. They develop industrial robots capable of welding and handling heavy objects.
1961: The first industrial robot, Unimate, is installed in a General Motors factory. It is capable of moving heavy metal parts and welding them
1986: Honda begins development of its bipedal robot project, which will lead to the creation of the ASIMO robot in the 2000s.
1997: NASA launches the Sojourner rover to Mars, one of the first robot explorers to land on another planet.
2002: Roomba, a robotic vacuum cleaner developed by iRobot, becomes the first domestic robot widely adopted by the general public.
2015: Boston Dynamics unveils Spot, a quadruped robot capable of moving autonomously over varied terrain, opening the way to applications in construction and surveillance.
2020: Robots are used for missions related to the COVID-19 pandemic, such as disinfecting public spaces or assisting hospitals.
